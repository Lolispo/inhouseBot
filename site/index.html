inhouseBot reads everyone in your Discord voice channel and splits them into the two most evenly-matched teams it can build. It tracks a rating for every player across seven games, runs a real competitive map veto, and pulls live kills, headshots and entry frags straight off the CS server the moment a match ends.

How it's built

A single Node + TypeScript service on discord.js drives the whole thing. State lives in MySQL through Sequelize — players, per-game ratings, matches and a full per-round CS stat sheet.

For Counter-Strike, the bot talks to a get5 server (Dathost) and ingests the end-of-match stat dump. For Dota, it opens a socket to a companion match-making app to create and follow games. Everything else — balancing, veto, MMR maths, moving people between voice channels — happens in the bot.
